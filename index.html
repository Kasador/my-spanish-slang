What is Slang?

Slang is considered very informal usage in vocabulary and can change regulary. Slang exists because of people themselves; using words and phrases that doesn't excatly mean what they're suppose to mean. Slang often changes throughout the years and usually is different and can vary between generations. Slang evoles with each generation and usually when talking to another person from a different gernation, they won't understand the same slang you know all to well. What are Sayings?

A saying is anything that is spoken or memorable that doesn't have a literal translation. For an example, I'm so hungry I can eat a horse, this saying doesn't literally mean that the person could eat a horse, but he or she is saying that they are super-duper hungry. Sayings usually are only understable to other native speakers of that language (unless you learn them of course). That is why sometimes it can sound weird in a different language but could totally make sense to the native speakers. For an example in Spanish, the saying, a lo hecho, pecho, meaning in the face of deeds done, present a full chest; however, it is the equivalent of the English saying, what's done is done. The Spanish Language

The Spanish language comes from los conquistadores, in Spain. However, because there are over 20 different Spanish-speaking countries, each individual country has its own slang and sayings that could mean one thing in one country but could mean something entirely different in another. This is why its particularly difficult to learn Spanish completely. This is why I put together this website to help more gringos learn Spanish and its slang.
